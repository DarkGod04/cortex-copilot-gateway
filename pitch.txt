                    CORTEX COPILOT - PROJECT PITCH & EXPLANATION
================================================================================

Hey! Welcome to the team. Let me walk you through what I have built. 

Imagine you are managing a massive industrial factory. Every month, you get a 
complicated electricity bill. You've got contracts specifying how much peak 
electricity (in kVA) you are allowed to draw, strict regulations about keeping 
your Power Factor (PF) close to 1.0 (to avoid massive grid penalties), and concerns 
about voltage harmonics (THD) ruining your heavy machinery.

To keep track of this, your team records thousands of sensor data points in a 
massive CSV file. But as a busy factory manager, you don't have the time to look 
at spreadsheets, run Excel formulas, or write Python script queries just to find 
out if you did something wrong.

That's where "Cortex Copilot" comes in. 

I built an AI-powered smart energy assistant for factory managers. You open the 
dashboard, and instead of digging through spreadsheets, you simply talk to it in 
plain English—like "What is my bill for June 2026?" or "Compare my June peak demand 
with May 2026"—and it gives you the exact numbers in seconds!

Here is how the magic works under the hood:

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. THE 3-STEP PIPELINE (THE BACKEND FLOW)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
When you send a message, it doesn't just feed the entire text directly to a general 
chatbot. Instead, it goes through a smart 3-step pipeline:

* STEP 1: Intent Routing (The Traffic Controller)
  We use a lightweight, local AI model called "phi3" running on your computer. 
  Its only job is to read your query and decide the "timeframe intent". 
  For example, if you ask "what was my peak demand on June 15?", it outputs:
  { "timeframe": "daily", "target_date": "2026-06-15" }
  It also understands comparison intents and extracts target vs comparison dates.

* STEP 2: Pandas Data ETL (The Calculator)
  Once the AI router tells us the timeframe, a Python backend script using the 
  "Pandas" library instantly opens the factory's CSV data file, slices it to 
  focus ONLY on that specific date or month, and calculates:
  - The total estimated bill (in Indian Rupees).
  - The maximum peak demand (in kVA) during that timeframe.
  - The average Power Factor.
  - Any active anomalies (e.g. if the demand exceeded contract limits).

* STEP 3: Response Generator (The Presenter)
  We inject these calculated numbers into a clean prompt context and pass it back 
  to the LLM. The AI then formats a direct, bullet-free, bolded metadata answer 
  such as:
  **Timeframe Scope:** Daily (2026-06-15)
  **Peak Demand:** 1002.75 kVA

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
2. THE SUPER COOL FEATURES
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
What makes this dashboard feel premium and interactive?

* Dynamic SVG Area Charts: 
  When the assistant returns timeseries metrics (like demand over time), it doesn't 
  just show text. It draws a custom, zero-dependency SVG area chart inline 
  inside the assistant's chat bubble! It adjusts dynamically to show hourly trends 
  for daily queries, daily trends for monthly queries, or monthly trends.

* Suggested Prompt Pills:
  Instead of staring at a blank chat input, the gateway checks what anomalies are 
  currently happening in your factory and dynamically suggests clickable pills 
  like "Why is my Peak Demand exceeding limits?" or "Show my average Power Factor trend".

* Real-Time WebSocket Alerts:
  The dashboard keeps a live, open connection (using WebSockets) to the server. 
  If a critical excursion happens (like a sudden drop in Power Factor or a Total 
  Harmonic Distortion peak), warning cards pop up dynamically inside the chat stream.

* Tenant Isolation (Security):
  We support multiple factories (Tenant A and Tenant B). The backend checks the 
  headers for every query and normalizes file paths to ensure a user from Tenant A 
  can never leak or see data belonging to Tenant B.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
4. HOW YOU CAN RUN IT LOCAL:
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
- Running Ollama locally: `ollama run phi3`
- Backend: Run `pip install -r requirements.txt` and start with `uvicorn app.main:app --reload`
- Frontend: Run `npm install` and start with `npm run dev`

And that's it! A secure, local AI energy analyst that saves factories millions 
in grid penalties through simple conversations.
